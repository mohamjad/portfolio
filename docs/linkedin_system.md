# LinkedIn System (Internal)

## Product Positioning Anchor
Setta is a paid creative modeling layer for beauty and DTC teams. Input is a campaign brief. Output is what to shoot, what to avoid, creator-fit lanes, and execution gates before performance compression.

## ICP Matrix

### Agency ICP
- Paid social agencies running beauty, skincare, cosmetics, or DTC accounts.
- Teams testing 10+ concepts per month and managing active creator pipelines.
- High urgency around creative fatigue and rapid iteration.

### Brand ICP
- In-house growth or paid social teams at beauty and DTC brands.
- US or Western-first launch plans with cross-market curiosity.
- Teams with enough media volume to benefit from fast kill/scale decisions.

## Title-Level Targeting Map

### Agency targets
- Paid Social Creative Director
- Performance Creative Strategist
- Creative Strategy Lead (Paid)
- UGC Campaign Manager
- Growth Creative Director

### Brand targets
- Paid Social Manager
- Performance Marketing Manager
- Growth Marketing Lead
- Creative Operations Manager (paid/performance focus)

## Qualification Rubric

### Fit score (0-10)
- Volume fit (10+ concepts/month): 0-3
- Paid urgency (active campaigns now): 0-3
- Creative decision pain (explicit testing friction): 0-2
- Cross-market relevance (US + JP/KR curiosity): 0-2

### Disqualifiers
- No paid creative testing process.
- Campaign volume below threshold with no near-term ramp.
- Looking only for one-off influencer sourcing with no performance goals.

## LinkedIn Profile Messaging Stack

### Headline formula
Campaign Brief In -> Format Decisions Out | Paid Creative Modeling for Beauty + DTC | What to Shoot, What to Avoid, Who Can Execute

### About structure
1. Problem: teams test formats too late.
2. Method: brief mapping to live format velocity and saturation.
3. Output: ranked bets, do-not-shoot, creator lanes, and test protocol.
4. Proof framing: sample briefs and explainable proof tiles.
5. CTA: send a campaign brief.

### Featured section order
1. Landing page.
2. LAKA sample brief.
3. VT sample brief.
4. Pricing snapshot.

## Process Notes
- Keep messaging decision-first, not dashboard-first.
- Lead with one specific sample relevant to prospect category.
- Use operational language only after qualification confirms paid testing volume.
