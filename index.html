01

System Health Evaluation

A lot of systems don't fail all at once. They look fine, numbers are green, and then something breaks in a way nobody saw coming.

I kept running into this while fixing broken automation and pipelines, so I built a way to reason about whether systems are actually healthy, not just passing checks on the surface.

What this helps with

This helps answer questions like:

  • Are things actually stable right now, or just not obviously broken?
  • What would fail next if usage or conditions change?
  • What signals would have caught the last incident earlier?

Instead of waiting for alerts after something breaks, it focuses on signals that change before the failure shows up.

How I approach it

I break systems down into:

  • signals that change early
  • failure modes that actually matter
  • incidents that already happened

When something breaks, I don't just fix it. I look at what should have caught it earlier and update the signals so the same thing doesn't happen again.

The part I care about most

The most useful part is being able to take a past incident and ask, "what would have caught this sooner?"

The demo lets you see which signals fired too late, which ones were missing entirely, and what to change so the system would've noticed earlier next time.

Why this exists

This grew out of fixing real systems. Revenue pipelines, monitoring setups, automation that quietly stopped working.

The other projects on this site are specific fixes. This is the way I think about preventing the same kinds of failures from happening again.

This isn't meant to be a finished product. It's how I reason about systems when I'm trying to figure out what's going to break next.
